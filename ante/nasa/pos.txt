particle words - words performing purely grammatical functions and have no semantic meaning.
content words - words performing purely semantic functions and have no grammatical meaning. 
prepositions - words that can establish a relationship to a prepositional phrase.
preverbs - words that can modify verbs in the opposite direction of regular word order.
conjunctions - words that can link concepts between multiple sentences together.
modifier particles -  words performing semantic functions while being exclusively used as modifiers.
interjections - words or clusters able to be said in isolation as an exclamation or remark.
loaned adjectives - loaned words that can act strictly as modifiers on native TP content words to name said concept in context.
